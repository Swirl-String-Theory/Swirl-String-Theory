
    \subsection{Persistence of the electron trefoil under atomic binding}
        \label{subsec:electron_trefoil_persistence_atomic_binding}

        \textbf{[DERIVED SST CONSTRAINT].}
        Once an electron carrier has closed into the trefoil class \(3_1\),
        smooth propagation through the vacuum and ordinary atomic binding do not
        change its knot type. The distinction is
        \begin{align}
            3_1^{\rm free}
            \longrightarrow
            3_1^{\rm bound},
            \label{eq:free_to_bound_trefoil}
        \end{align}
        not
        \begin{align}
            3_1 \longrightarrow 0_1,
            \qquad
            3_1 \longrightarrow \emptyset .
        \end{align}
        Ordinary atomic capture is therefore a binding and phase-locking process,
        not a topological breaking process.

        The bound electron is written as
        \begin{align}
            e^-_{\rm atom}
            =
            3_1^{\rm core}
            \otimes
            \Psi_{n\ell m\sigma}^{\rm envelope},
            \label{eq:bound_electron_core_envelope}
        \end{align}
        where \(3_1^{\rm core}\) denotes the protected topological carrier and
        \(\Psi_{n\ell m\sigma}^{\rm envelope}\) denotes the bound atomic
        phase/swirl envelope. The invariant data retained under ordinary binding are
        \begin{align}
            K=3_1,
            \qquad
            \Gamma=\Gamma_0,
        \end{align}
        while the embedding, dressing, and phase envelope may change.

        \textbf{[SPECULATIVE SST BRIDGE].}
        Destruction or conversion of the external electron-trefoil sector is
        admissible only through a non-Euler process such as annihilation with an
        anti-trefoil or weak-sector capture,
        \begin{align}
            3_1+\overline{3_1} &\longrightarrow \gamma+\gamma,\\
            3_1+K_p &\longrightarrow K_n+\nu_e+\mathcal{R}.
            \label{eq:trefoil_conversion_bridges}
        \end{align}
        These reactions are not ordinary elastic atomic binding events.
