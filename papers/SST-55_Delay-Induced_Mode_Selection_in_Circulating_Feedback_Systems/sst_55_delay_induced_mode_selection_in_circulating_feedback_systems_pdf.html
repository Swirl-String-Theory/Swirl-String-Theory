Delay-Induced Mode Selection

Interactive simulation of spectral discreteness in circulating feedback loops based on Iskandarani (2026).
Physics Insight:
A finite delay ($\tau$) in a feedback loop acts as a filter. Even without spatial boundaries, the delay enforces a discrete "ladder" of stable frequencies ($\Omega$).

Equation: $\Omega = \omega_0 - \kappa \sin(\Omega\tau)$
