Manuscript title: Delay-Induced Mode Selection in Circulating Feedback Systems

Dear Editor,

I am submitting the above manuscript to AIP Advances via the AIP transfer pathway from Chaos: An Interdisciplinary Journal of Nonlinear Science (Manuscript #CHA26-AR-00183). The Chaos editorial decision dated 02-Feb-2026 recommended transfer to AIP Advances on scope/fit grounds.

The manuscript develops a reduced delay-differential phase model for circulating feedback loops and shows that finite transit time alone can enforce a discrete set of stable operating frequencies through dynamical stability selection, without invoking spatial standing-wave boundary conditions. The result provides a compact design-relevant mechanism applicable across physical platforms where looped propagation and feedback are present (e.g., optical/electronic ring systems, delayed control loops, and distributed oscillator networks).

Related submission disclosure. This manuscript is conceptually related to a separate manuscript of mine currently under review at AIP Advances titled “Delay-Induced Pattern Formation as a Route to Mode Discreteness in Nonlinear Ring Systems.” The companion paper treats spatiotemporal pattern formation in extended/nonlocal ring settings, whereas the present manuscript isolates the minimal phase-locked circulating mode-selection mechanism in a reduced effective model. The two manuscripts have distinct primary results and are intended to be complementary rather than overlapping.

This work has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration by any other journal. No external funding supported this research. Data sharing is not applicable because no new datasets were created or analyzed.
