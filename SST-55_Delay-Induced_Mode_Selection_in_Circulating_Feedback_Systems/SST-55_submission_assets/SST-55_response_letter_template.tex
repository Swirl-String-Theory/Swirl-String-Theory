    \noindent Thank you for the editorial assessment of my submission to \emph{Chaos} (MS \#CHA26-AR-00183).
    The decision letter dated 02-Feb-2026 indicated that, while the manuscript was not a fit for \emph{Chaos}'s
    multidisciplinary scope, it may be suitable for a more specialized venue and recommended transfer to
    \emph{AIP Advances}. I am therefore submitting this work to \emph{AIP Advances} via the AIP transfer pathway.

    \medskip
    \noindent \textbf{Nature of the decision and available reports.}
    The \emph{Chaos} decision was editorial and did not include external referee reports. Accordingly, there are no
    point-by-point reviewer responses to provide; this document addresses the scope/fit issue raised in the editorial
    decision and summarizes changes made to align the presentation with \emph{AIP Advances}.

    \medskip
    \noindent \textbf{Scope alignment for \emph{AIP Advances}.}
    In preparing the manuscript for \emph{AIP Advances}, I revised the framing and discussion to emphasize the paper's
    relevance to delayed-feedback physics in circulating systems (delay-differential phase dynamics, stability-selected
    frequency ladders, and implications for looped photonic/electronic/control platforms), rather than positioning the
    result primarily as a broad cross-disciplinary contribution.

    \medskip
    \noindent \textbf{Summary of revisions since the \emph{Chaos} submission.}
    A clean revised manuscript and a marked-up version are provided. Key updates include:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Clarified the phase-locked condition and its geometric interpretation as intersections of $\Omega$ with
        $\omega_0-\kappa\sin(\Omega\tau)$; cleaned the accompanying figure and caption for readability.
        \item Expanded the linear stability derivation and stated the stability selection inequality
        $1+\kappa\tau\cos(\Omega\tau)>0$ explicitly; added a stability-map figure and clarified the ``stable branch''
        interpretation.
        \item Strengthened the discussion to present the mechanism as a classical delay-induced spectral selection effect
        (distinct from standing-wave cavity quantization), and positioned it in an effective-theory perspective.
        \item Minor edits: tightened definitions, improved notation consistency, corrected typos, and added citations where appropriate.
    \end{itemize}
